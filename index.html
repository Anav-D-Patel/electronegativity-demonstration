What you are seeing: A simulation of a chemical bond. Two atoms are playing a "tug-of-war" for a cloud of shared electrons. Created by Anav, Martin, Dorian and Devan

Electronegativity Difference (ΔEN): Electronegativity measures how strongly an atom pulls on electrons. The difference (ΔEN) between the two atoms determines how the electrons are shared.

What to do: Try choosing different elements from the dropdown menus (or use the custom sliders). Watch how changing the ΔEN shifts the electron cloud, pulling the bond from Nonpolar Covalent, to Polar Covalent, and eventually to an Ionic bond where the electrons are entirely transfered!

New: Try selecting two metals and see what happens — metal-metal interactions follow completely different rules!

⚙️ Metal + Metal = Metallic Bond!

When two metals interact, they don't share or transfer electrons the way covalent or ionic bonds do. Instead, they form a metallic bond.

In a metallic bond, the metal atoms release their outer (valence) electrons into a shared "sea of electrons" that flows freely around a lattice of positively charged metal ions. No single atom owns the electrons — they belong to the whole structure!

This is why metals are:

  • 🔌 Good conductors — electrons flow freely
  • 🔨 Malleable & ductile — ions can slide without breaking bonds
  • ✨ Shiny — free electrons reflect light

The electronegativity difference (ΔEN) between two metals is usually very small, but the bond type is not nonpolar covalent — it's a fundamentally different kind of bond!
